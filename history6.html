Alsvin

..

‘Alsvin’ means ‘very swift’ and it was the very appropriate name given to one of the two horses responsible for pulling the sun across the sky in a celestial chariot driven by the sun goddess, Sol.

Alsvin


Alsvin, sometimes spelt ‘Alsvior’, was aided in his task of pulling the sun chariot by Arvakr, whose name translates as ‘Early riser’. Understandably, you may have some trouble swallowing the notion of horses pulling the sun through the sky. However, as it turns out the gods had it covered and attached cooling iron bellows to the horses’ shoulders so that they wouldn’t burn up.

Both of the horses played another important role too. Today, we commonly associate the sun with providing us with both heat and light but in Norse mythology, the sun provided just the heat, while the shimmering manes of Alsvin and Arvakr generated the daylight.
